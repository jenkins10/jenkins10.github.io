Experience

Professional Background

July 2015 — Present

Senior Professional Staff

JHU Applied Physics Laboratory — Space Exploration Sector

  • Electro-Optical Terrain Sensing Function Lead for the Mobility subsystem on NASA's Dragonfly mission to Titan. Led a team of four engineers through full lifecycle development of an onboard optical navigation algorithm for autonomous rotorcraft flight.
  • Managed software development using agile practices and CI/CD pipelines, delivering multiple tested code releases to the flight software baseline.
  • Architected a cross-domain software/firmware distributed algorithm spanning GN&C, flight software, and firmware teams — optimizing resource utilization while maintaining algorithmic flexibility.
  • Navigation Engineer for SMART Nav on NASA's DART mission. Designed and implemented the optical targeting algorithm that autonomously discriminated the 160-meter moon Dimorphos from the primary asteroid Didymos during the terminal approach phase, contributing to mission success in September 2022.
  • Principal Investigator for Autonomous Station Keeping research — designing onboard guidance and navigation algorithms for spacecraft operating in chaotic dynamical environments near small bodies.
  • Architected a modular, extensible software framework for rapid analysis of mission concepts requiring autonomous onboard navigation and trajectory planning.
